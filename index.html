Preference elicitation questionnaire

A decision problem is not one-dimensional and straightforward. The reality is that a decision problem is multifaceted and complex; it often involves multiple criteria, preference rankings and selection. In day-to-day life, we people often make decisions based on intuition and without having a real idea why a decision alternative is selected.

However, complex decision-making will entail some conflict between several goals, objectives, or criteria. In this questionnaire, you will be asked to provide your preferences in a multi-criteria decision situation. Below you will be presented with a decision-making situation where you will get the chance to interact with a ratio weight elicitation method called SMART, a rank order weight elicitation method, and the analytical hierarchy process (AHP).

In this study, we aim to evaluate how these weight elicitation methods are perceived by examining aspects such as prior knowledge of decision analysis, cognitive demand of the method, time efficiency, stability of preferences and subject information usability.

Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP)

  The AHP is a pairwise comparison method meaning that in this method, alternatives are compared pairwise. This systematic pairwise comparison method can be used to elicit preferences, by the decision-maker first choosing between two criteria. Using a semantic scale (e.g., “less important”, “important”, “equally important”, “more important” etc), the decision-maker can determine the strength of the ordering (I.e., how much more important the first criterion is compared to the other). These steps are then repeated for the remaining criterion.
